The figure above represents the different historical experiences of U.S. counties with anti-black human rights violations; 5 distinct patterns have been identified

Over time, a great number of research projects have emerged to document as well as understand what coercion and force has been directed against African Americans. For example, Mapping Police Violence considers the behavior of law enforcement. Stewart Tolnay and E.M. (Wood) Beck as well as later the National Museum for Peace and Justice focus on lynchings. Some focus on "outrages" anti-black riots that occurred after slavery. Pauli Murray focus on political and civil liberties restrictions. Davenport, Soule and Armstrong focus on the protest policing of black protest events. This represents only a few efforts.

While useful for establishing a record of what has transpired, it is clear that a great many coercive and forceful activities were directed against African Americans that were not well documented and that the research in this field has only scratched the surface. Most researchers have been myopically focused on a single form of activity and there have been very few efforts to try and pull all of the different activities together in one place. The current project seeks to do just this. Our objective is to create the most comprehensive measure of anti-black human rights violations yet compiled.

How will we do this? We use different data that has been compiled by ourselves as well as other individuals over the last twenty years. This effort was advanced by a grant from the Research Council Norway. In addition to this, in the future we will create original data of our own design taking advantage of new source material and/or new text extraction techniques.

On this webpage, we will describe how we compile what we do, how we create our measure and reveal some interesting patterns that have emerged. We introduce our interactive webpage where one can look up individual counties to see behavior/laws at a particular period of time or over time. Here one will also be able to compare across locales as well as gauge the importance of distinct county-level characteristics. More information and analyses are following.

On this webpage, we will also introduce ourselves and provide a blog to highlight project developments as well as to discuss other topics that we find relevant.

Christian Davenport & Dave Armstrong
